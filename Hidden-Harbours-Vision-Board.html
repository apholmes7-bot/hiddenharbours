What the game is about

Five Design Pillars

Every feature has to serve at least one of these. They're the soul of the game and the tie-breaker for every decision.

1

The Sea Has Moods

Tide, wind, weather and the 24-hour clock are real forces you read and respect. The same cove is a different place at high water than at low, calm than gale.

2

From Dory to Dynasty

A long, legible ladder of scale — start hauling line by hand in a borrowed dory, end directing a fleet and shipping freight. Growth you can see.

3

A Living Working Coast

NPCs keep routines, the town runs to its own rhythm, the market breathes with supply and demand. The island is inhabited and reactive, with or without you.

4

Earn It, Then Automate It

Do every job by hand first, so it has weight. Then hire crew and build infrastructure to automate the tedium — shifting from labourer to owner.

5

Cozy, but with Teeth

Stardew warmth, but the sea is genuinely dangerous: run aground, capsize, lose a load, get lost in fog. Risk is what gives the coziness its stakes.

What makes it special

Signature Systems

🌊 Tides & the 24h Clock

A real semidiurnal tide on a spring/neap cycle. Low tide opens the seabed and strands deep boats; high tide floods the flats. A tide table you learn to read is a core tool.

🧭 Wind & Navigation

Wind and tidal current pull physically on the hull. Heavier boats carry more momentum and are harder to turn and stop. Navigation is a skill, not a button.

⚠️ Danger & Rescue

Misread the depth and you ground. Overload in a gale and you broach. Break down offshore and you're stranded, exposed, until a tow comes — for a price.

⚖️ A Market That Breathes

Supply and demand at Greywick. Land too much cod and the price craters; scarcity makes it spike. Store it, time it, refine it, or ship it somewhere hungrier.

👥 Staff & Automation

Do it by hand to learn it, then hire crew to run routes, process catch, and sell — turning the daily grind into a business you steer with policy.

🏚️ Homes & Holdings

Begin in your late uncle's cottage. Buy, upgrade and refurnish homes — then warehouses, processing plants and berths as the enterprise grows.

🐟 100 Fish, as Data

A hundred species gated by region, depth, tide, time, season and weather — built as data so the catalogue can grow without touching code.

🗺️ Authored + Simulated

Handcrafted regions and a named cast give the world its soul; simulated weather, spawns and crowds give it endless, lawful variety.

Look, feel & lineage

Art Direction

¾ top-down pixel art (Stardew's readable structure) wrapped in the painterly parallax, fog and lantern-light of Kingdoms Two Crowns. One perspective for land, town and sea. A consistent scale — 32 px to the metre — so the size fantasy reads true.

North Atlantic palette

Slate seas and fog greys, weathered wood and sand — with saturated pops from lobster buoys, oilskins and painted hulls. The palette cools and darkens out toward Ironbound, and desaturates into the Smother.
